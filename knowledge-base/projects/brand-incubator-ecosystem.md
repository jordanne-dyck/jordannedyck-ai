# Brand Incubator Ecosystem

## Executive Summary

Building an independent yet integrated tech ecosystem that enables DECIEM to rapidly launch and test new brand concepts on e-commerce with minimal investment in time and money. Simple enough for small teams to operate without impacting The Ordinary's enterprise workflows, yet structured to scale when brands grow.

**Key Innovation**: Tiered architecture that maintains startup agility for new brands while preserving integration path to enterprise systems as brands mature.

**Status**: Currently in development. Testing model and architecture with pilot brand launches.

## The Problem & Opportunity

**Strategic Challenge:**
- DECIEM is a brand portfolio (multiple beauty brands beyond The Ordinary)
- The Ordinary runs on enterprise Salesforce Commerce Cloud ($150M+ revenue, complex workflows)
- New brand concepts need to launch quickly and cheaply to test market fit
- Can't use enterprise platform for experiments (too expensive, too slow, too complex)
- Can't build completely separate systems (no integration path, siloed data, operational chaos)

**The Opportunity**: Build tiered system where new brands start independent and simple, but can graduate to enterprise platform seamlessly as they scale.

## Solution Design

### Tiered Architecture

**Design Principles:**
- **Independent**: New brands don't bottleneck on The Ordinary's Salesforce workflows
- **Integrated**: Data flows between systems when needed (inventory, customer, analytics)
- **Simple**: Small teams (1-3 people) can launch and operate brands
- **Scalable**: Clear graduation path as brands grow

**Three-Tier Model:**

**Tier 1: Concept Testing (0-6 months)**
- Platform: Shopify (experimenting with Shopify vs. Salesforce for more flexibility)
- Cost: $500-1K/month
- Launch: 2-4 weeks with 1-2 people
- Use: Validate product-market fit, test messaging

**Tier 2: Growth Phase (6-24 months, $1M-5M revenue)**
- Platform: Shopify Plus
- Cost: $2K-5K/month (scales with revenue)
- Upgrade: Days, not months
- Use: Scale validated concepts, expand operations

**Tier 3: Enterprise Scale ($5M+ revenue)**
- Platform: Salesforce Commerce Cloud (join The Ordinary)
- Migration: 3-6 months
- Use: Mature brands needing enterprise capabilities

**Shared Services:**
- Email/SMS, customer support, analytics, fulfillment
- Unified customer data and inventory visibility across brands
- Independent product catalogs, marketing, and day-to-day operations

**Result**: Launch in 4 weeks vs. 6+ months, operate with minimal resources, scale when ready.

## Expected Outcomes

**Speed:**
- Time to market: 6 months → 4 weeks (6x faster)
- Brand experiments per year: 1-2 → 4-6
- Iteration velocity: Weekly vs. quarterly

**Cost Efficiency:**
- Launch cost: $75K-150K → $10K-20K (85-90% reduction)
- Monthly operating cost: $15K-30K → $1K-3K
- Cost of failed experiments: $150K+ → <$25K

**Operational Simplicity:**
- Team size: 5-10 people → 1-3 people
- Technical complexity: High → Low
- Bottleneck on enterprise team: Yes → No

**Strategic Flexibility:**
- Test multiple brand concepts simultaneously
- Learn quickly, fail fast
- Scale winners to enterprise when ready
- Portfolio approach to brand building

## Challenges & Learnings

### What We're Testing

**Technical Architecture:**
- Data synchronization patterns between Shopify and Salesforce
- Customer identity management across brands
- Inventory allocation and visibility
- Analytics aggregation for portfolio view

**Operational Model:**
- Governance without bureaucracy
- Brand independence while maintaining consistency
- Resource sharing without bottlenecks
- Decision-making frameworks for tier graduation

**Business Questions:**
- What metrics trigger graduation to next tier?
- How much integration is optimal?
- What can/should be shared vs. independent?
- How to maintain brand differentiation?

### Early Insights

**What's Working:**
- Tier-based approach allows appropriate complexity per stage
- Template-based launches dramatically reduce time
- Small teams move faster with less coordination overhead
- Shopify's simplicity enables non-technical operation

**Open Challenges:**
- Data synchronization requires careful design
- Balancing independence with integration
- Managing multiple tech stacks organizationally
- Defining clear graduation criteria

**Key Learnings:**
- Start simple, add complexity only when needed
- Independence is more valuable than integration early on
- Template everything possible
- Clear decision gates prevent premature optimization

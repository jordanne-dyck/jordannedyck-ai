# Digital Transformation Salesforce

## Executive Summary

Led DECIEM's digital transformation from DIY custom-built e-commerce platform to enterprise-grade Salesforce DTC ecosystem, enabling 100+ new features, tools and functionality, creating centralized DTC data and operating model supporting 150+ countries globally and $150M+ in annual revenue.

**Key Achievement**: Strategic buy vs. build decision enabled team to focus on innovation rather than infrastructure, providing scalable foundation for AI capabilities and rapid growth.

## The Problem

### DIY Platform Couldn't Scale

**Technical Reality:**
- **Custom-built platform**: In-house developed e-commerce system not built for enterprise scale
- **Level of effort**: Building all needed functionality from scratch would take years
- **Growing pains**: 80% YoY growth exposing platform limitations daily
- **Engineering bottleneck**: Team spending time maintaining infrastructure vs. building features
- **Scalability crisis**: Platform struggling under increasing traffic and 150+ country complexity
- **Lack of business tools**: In-house developed e-commerce system lacked fundamental business user features leading to reliance on development team for simple tasks (ie. merchandising, content managemnent, reporting/ analytics, marketing campaigns, etc.)

**Strategic Choice:**
Instead of spending years building everything from scratch to catch up, invest in proven enterprise platform. Focus engineering team on innovation and differentiation, not commodity infrastructure.

## Solution Design

### Buy vs. Build Decision

**Why Salesforce:**
- Proven scalability for $150M+ revenue across 150+ countries and multiple brands
- Native integration with Marketing Cloud, Service Cloud, Data Cloud
- Unified customer data across all touchpoints
- Team can build unique differentiating brand expereinces and features, not infrastructure

**Centralized DTC Operating Model:**
- Single platform for all global markets
- Integrated enterprise tools (commerce, marketing, service, analytics)
- Unified customer record globally
- API-first architecture for flexibility
- Foundation for AI and personalization

**Approach:**
- MVP rollout starting with highest priority features and functionality
- SEO mitigation strategy (301 redirects, monitoring, quick corrections)
- Required global launch due to legacy set up
- Team upskilling and change management
- 8-month transformation timeline

## Challenges & Learnings

### What Worked Well

✅ **Buy vs. build decision**: Avoided years of development, accelerated innovation
✅ **Integration layer**: API-first approach enabled flexibility

### Challenges Overcome

**SEO Impact from Domain Changes**
- Problem: Domain changes impact search rankings
- Solution: 301 redirects, daily monitoring, quick corrections
- Result: Revenue impact minimized, rankings recovered

**Project Management**
- Problem: Working with multiple vendors focused on parts of the solution led to functionality gaps cross-cloud
- Solution: Transitioned to one SI partner post migration
- Result: Complete solutions delivered, simpler project planning, streamlined devOps

**Team Skills Gap**
- Problem: Developmnet team managed most operations histrically, leading to skills gap for business users
- Solution: Training program, hired experts, paired learning
- Result: Team fully proficient within 6 months

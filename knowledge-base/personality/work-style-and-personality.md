# Work Style Summary

I thrive in **fast-paced, ambiguous environments** where we're figuring things out as we go. Give me complex problems, data access, customers, and a talented team—I'll find innovative paths forward through creative problem-solving.

**My Ideal Environment:**
- High-growth tech companies (startup or mid-size)
- Organizations embracing AI transformation as core mission
- **Fun, collaborative culture** with passionate people who care deeply about their work
- **Human-centered workplace** valuing equality, fairness, belonging, health, and flexibility
- Results-oriented culture measuring impact over activity
- Cross-functional collaboration with autonomy to execute
- Direct access to customers and performance data
- Teams valuing both strategic thinking AND hands-on building
- Places where **kindness and high performance coexist**

**Not A Good Fit:**
- Slow, bureaucratic environments with heavy process
- Organizations where politics matter more than merit
- Roles doing the same thing repeatedly
- Places resistant to change or innovation
- Cultures where consensus matters more than clarity
- Environments that sacrifice people's wellbeing for results
- Teams that don't value collaboration or mutual respect

# Leadership & Decision-Making Philosophy

**Inspiring & Influential Leadership:**
I lead through energy, ideas, and genuine care for people. I'm known for inspiring teams to think bigger, move faster, and believe in what we're building. My influence comes from demonstrating what's possible, not from authority. I energize people around a vision and make them excited to be part of something meaningful.

**How I Operate:**
I identify opportunities, test hypotheses rapidly, scale what works, and kill what doesn't. Focus on finding white space for innovation.

**People-First Philosophy:**
Great people make great products. I prioritize:
- **Equality & Fairness**: Everyone's voice matters; decisions based on merit, not hierarchy
- **Belonging**: Creating environments where people feel safe to be themselves, contribute authentically, and bring their whole selves to work
- **Health & Wellbeing**: Mental and physical health aren't nice-to-haves—they're foundational. Burned out teams don't innovate
- **Flexibility**: Trust people to manage their time and work in ways that suit their lives. Results matter, not where or when work happens
- **Human & Kind**: Being successful doesn't require being ruthless. Kindness, empathy, and treating people well creates better outcomes

**Core Leadership Principles:**
- **Empowerment Over Control**: Hire great people, give them space to do great work. Provide clear objectives and context, trust the team on execution
- **Bias Toward Action**: Build prototypes and test quickly vs. months of planning. Ship, measure, learn, improve, repeat
- **Data-Informed, Not Paralyzed**: Every decision informed by data, but don't wait for perfect information. Gather enough, move forward, adjust
- **Radical Transparency**: Share information openly—what we know, don't know, what we're learning. Encourage direct, candid feedback. Avoid office politics

**What I Value in Teams:**
Urgency (move fast, make decisions) • Directness (say what you mean) • Curiosity (always learning) • Ownership (take full responsibility) • Collaboration (help each other succeed) • Customer focus (every decision connects to value) • Creative thinking (challenge assumptions) • Kindness (treat people well) • Passion (care deeply about the work)

# Communication & Collaboration Style

**Direct Communication:**
I give and appreciate straight talk. If something isn't working, I'll say it clearly but respectfully. No passive-aggressive communication or office politics.

**Storytelling:**
I synthesize complex information into compelling narratives—whether presenting to executives or explaining technical concepts to non-technical stakeholders. Focus on making information accessible and actionable.

**Context-Rich:**
I provide the "why" behind decisions, not just the "what." I want people to understand reasoning so they can make good decisions independently.

**Meeting Philosophy:**
Default to async communication (Slack, docs, videos). When we meet, have clear agendas and make decisions. I excel at facilitating workshops and strategy sprints that drive alignment.

**Working With Me:**
- **As Team Member**: Autonomous (don't need check-ins), reliable (commitments get done), helpful (unblock others), curious (ask lots of questions), direct (give/receive candid feedback), supportive (celebrate wins, learn from failures together)
- **As Leader**: Inspiring (energize people around vision), influential (drive change through ideas and energy), objective-focused (clear on what, flexible on how), delegating (trust people), teaching (invest in growth), protective (shield from chaos), transparent (share context), **human and kind** (care about people as people, not just resources)
- **As Collaborator**: Bridge technical and business teams, facilitate alignment workshops, help teams see connections, drive adoption of new ways of working, create psychological safety for experimentation

**Bilingual:**
Fluent in English and French, able to work across languages and cultures.

# Problem-Solving Approach

**Creative:**
I approach challenges from multiple angles and combine ideas from different domains in novel ways. Challenge conventions, take calculated risks.

**Customer-First:**
Every decision connects to customer value. If it doesn't help customers, why are we building it?

**Data-Driven:**
I leverage analytics, user research, and testing to inform strategy, but balance data with intuition and move forward even with imperfect information.

**AI-Native Thinking:**
I consider how AI can fundamentally transform experiences, not just optimize existing processes.

**Lean Execution:**
Fast iteration over perfect planning. Execute through user research, workshops, testing, prioritization, design sprints, development, and launch.

# What Energizes & Drains Me

**Energizes Me:**
- **Building**: Taking ideas from concept to reality, seeing people use what I've created
- **Rapid Learning**: Constantly learning in quickly shifting landscapes—new technologies, changing behaviors, emerging business models
- **Complex Problems**: At the intersection of technology, business strategy, and customer experience
- **Measurable Impact**: 30% YoY growth, significant cost savings, improved customer experiences
- **Working with Passionate People**: Team members who care deeply, bring energy, and push each other to be better
- **Fun & Collaborative Environment**: Laughing together, celebrating wins, supporting each other through challenges
- **Making People's Lives Better**: Through better products, better work environments, or better processes

**Drains Me:**
- **Slow Movement**: Static environments, routine work, long approval chains, analysis paralysis
- **Bureaucracy**: Process for process's sake, compliance theater, meetings about meetings
- **Lack of Autonomy**: Micromanagement, needing approval for every small decision
- **Politics**: Hidden agendas, passive-aggressive communication, decisions based on politics vs. what's best
- **No Customer Access**: Building in a vacuum without feedback or customer insights
- **Resistance to Innovation**: "Always done it this way" mentality, fear of trying new approaches, incremental thinking only
- **Toxic Environments**: Lack of respect, inequality, unfairness, disregard for people's wellbeing
- **Apathy**: People who don't care about the work or each other

# Areas I'm Actively Working On

**Patience & Pacing:**
I can be impatient when I see a clear path forward. My drive for speed sometimes means not everyone moves at my pace. I'm working on bringing people along, building in alignment time, recognizing that patience often leads to better outcomes.

*Positive flip*: My impatience stems from a bias toward action and results. I get energized by progress and shipping. I'm channeling this energy to bring teams along rather than leaving them behind.

**Context Gathering:**
I need sufficient context to build effective solutions. With limited background or unclear objectives, I'm less effective. I'm working on being explicit about context needs upfront, asking more questions early, building discovery phases, getting comfortable saying "I need to understand X before I can solve for Y."

*Positive flip*: My need for context stems from solving the RIGHT problem the RIGHT way. This thoroughness leads to better outcomes and fewer false starts.

**Need for Rapid Evolution:**
I get bored when things move slowly or become routine. Static environments drain my energy. I'm working on seeking high-change environments, injecting innovation in stable contexts, recognizing when I need new challenges, structuring a mix of execution and exploration.

*Positive flip*: My need for constant evolution means I stay sharp, current, and ahead of trends. In rapidly changing fields like AI, this restlessness is an asset—I thrive where others struggle with pace of change.

**Managing Ambiguity vs. Creating Clarity:**
I thrive in ambiguity, but sometimes underestimate how uncomfortable it is for others. What feels like exciting exploration to me can feel chaotic to teammates who prefer structure. I'm working on creating explicit frameworks in fuzzy spaces, communicating frequently about what we know/don't know, balancing flexibility with clear plans.

*Positive flip*: My comfort with ambiguity allows me to lead where others find overwhelming. I see possibilities where others see chaos.

# How to Work With Me

**To Get the Best From Me:**
- **Give me context**: The problem, constraints, stakeholders, success criteria
- **Be direct**: Candid feedback and straight talk
- **Move with urgency**: Clear timelines and momentum
- **Trust the process**: I may take unconventional paths, but I focus on outcomes
- **Embrace iteration**: Ship, learn, improve rather than wait for perfection
- **Challenge me**: I want to be pushed; bring different perspectives

**What I'm Actively Working On:**
- Being more patient with process and consensus-building
- Gathering sufficient context before jumping to solutions
- Creating clarity and structure for others navigating ambiguity
- Recognizing when others need more time or structure than I do

I believe the best leaders are self-aware about their growth edges and actively work on them. These aren't weaknesses to hide—they're opportunities to become more effective.

# Outside of Work & What I'm Looking For

**Outside Work:**
- **Music**: Play drums, guitar, sing
- **Building Things**: Woodworking, home renovations, DIY projects—love making something physical
- **Outdoors**: Hiking, climbing, skatboarding, snowboarding, exploring nature

These activities translate into how I approach work: always looking for new paths, not afraid of difficult terrain, finding rhythm in chaos, creating something tangible.

**Culture Fit - I Thrive In Organizations That:**
Value shipping over perfection • Trust their people • Are open to new ideas • Move quickly and make decisions • Give direct feedback • Measure what matters • **Prioritize equality and fairness** • **Create belonging for everyone** • **Care about people's health and wellbeing** • **Value collaboration and kindness** • **Are fun to work at** • **Attract passionate people**

**Culture Fit - I Struggle In Organizations That:**
Move slowly due to bureaucracy • Prioritize politics over merit • Don't empower individuals • Avoid difficult conversations • Value consensus over clarity • Resist change • Focus only on incremental improvement • **Have toxic or cutthroat cultures** • **Don't care about work-life balance** • **Treat people as resources, not humans** • **Have unfair or inequitable practices**

**What I'm Looking For Next:**
I'm seeking opportunities that combine:
- **Rapid evolution** where I'm constantly learning
- **A team** that moves fast and thinks creatively
- **Passionate, collaborative people** who care deeply about their work
- **Human-centered culture** valuing equality, belonging, health, flexibility, and kindness
- **AI transformation** in meaningful, impactful ways
- **Building something transformative** in spaces being reshaped by emerging technology
- **Impact-driven culture** measuring outcomes over activity
- **Fun environment** where people enjoy working together

I want my comfort with chaos and bias toward action to be assets that drive the mission forward—not liabilities to be managed. And I want to do it with people I genuinely enjoy working with, in an environment that values both high performance and human wellbeing.
